# Interview Flow

The skill has two modes:

- live interview
- transcript analysis

## Live Interview Mode

Do not ask all questions at once.

Move through six rounds.

### Round 1: Business Context

Goal:

Understand who the customer is, what business they do, and which offer or user group should be analyzed first.

Ask:

- What business do you mainly do now?
- Which offer or service do you want to grow most?
- Which user group do you want to analyze first?
- Where do your current customers usually come from?

Extract:

- industry
- business type
- main offer
- analysis scope
- current customer source

### Round 2: Customer Source

Goal:

Find real customer evidence.

Ask:

- Where did your past paying customers come from?
- What were the last three paying customers like?
- What do your smoothest customers have in common?
- What do your most difficult customers have in common?

Extract:

- proven customer sources
- real transaction types
- early core users
- early unfit users

### Round 3: User Types

Goal:

Separate four user types.

Ask:

- Who may need you, but has not realized it yet?
- Who is the user you should serve first?
- Who has stronger payment ability, cooperation quality, or referral value?
- Who looks like a customer but is not suitable?

Extract:

- potential users
- core users
- high-value users
- unfit users

### Round 4: Demand Scenes

Goal:

Understand what triggers the need.

Ask:

- In what situation do users usually come to you?
- What happened before they came to you?
- How do users describe the problem in their own words?
- What are they really afraid of?
- What happens if they do not solve it?

Extract:

- trigger scenarios
- surface problems
- deep pains
- risk consequences

### Round 5: Trust And Decision

Goal:

Understand why users trust, hesitate, buy, or leave.

Ask:

- What does the user care about at first contact?
- Why do users trust you?
- Why do users hesitate?
- Why do some users not buy?
- Why do users finally decide to buy or cooperate?

Extract:

- trust sources
- trust barriers
- decision barriers
- purchase triggers

### Round 6: Result And Referral

Goal:

Understand what makes users satisfied and willing to recommend.

Ask:

- When do users feel they have gained something?
- What makes users most satisfied?
- When do users repurchase?
- When do users refer you to others?
- How would users describe you to a friend?

Extract:

- result perception
- repurchase reasons
- referral reasons
- referral language

## Transcript Analysis Mode

Before generating outputs:

1. Read the transcript.
2. Extract all usable facts.
3. Judge information quality.
4. Ask follow-up questions if needed.

Information quality:

- `high`: enough evidence to generate complete profiles and lifecycle
- `medium`: usable, but missing parts must be marked
- `low`: too vague, ask follow-up before generating

If follow-up is needed, ask 3-8 questions only.

Prioritize missing information in this order:

1. analysis scope
2. core users
3. real transaction examples
4. user pains
5. trust and decision details
